podcasts

Podcasts are conversation fuel. Here are my favorites.

Common Sense

The same Dan Carlin from Hardcore History also hosts a political podcast where he opines on current events. He described himself as a 'political Martian' and is always engaging to listen to, even and especially if you don't agree with him.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Common Sense 316 – The Day of the Dove

EconTalk with Russ Roberts

Russ Roberts describes himself as a proponent of 'classical economic liberalism', and it's through that lens he interviews authors, laywers, economists from all politcal backgrounds. He has civil discussions with those with whom he disagrees and is a clear explicator of the Smithian worldview.

Topic: Economics

Key Episode: Matt Ridley on the Evolution of Everything

Freakonomics

Another highly popular podcast. Hosts Steven Dubner and Stephen Levitt 'explore the riddles of everyday life and the weird wrinkles of human nature — from cheating and crime to parenting and sports.'

Topic: Economics

Key Episode: Is America Ready for a “No-Lose Lottery”?

Fresh Air

Terry Gross interviews every sort of person and every conceivable topic. Authors, actors, journalists, politicians, scientists, astronauts. We should all aspire to achieve something in our lives noteworth enough to be interviewed by Terry Gross.

Topic: Culture

Key Episode: David Sedaris

Hardcore History

Host Dan Carlin breathes fire into history, telling stories from a fresh and brutally vivid perspective. He's the necessary corrective to the stale history classes we were all subjected to in high school. His 10 hour series on World War 1 was more gripping than any television show or film I've seen on the subject.

Topic: History

Key Episode: Hardcore History 50 – Blueprint for Armageddon I

Intelligence Matters with Michael Morell

Former acting Director of the CIA Michael Morell talks about security, privacy, and counter-terrorism efforts with top leaders of the U.S. intelligence community. This podcast gives a window into what conversations about counter-terrorism sound like from within the CIA.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Robert Cardillo on the NGA’s role in the raid on Osama bin Laden’s compound

Lawfare Podcast

A dense and technical discussion on current events, with a focus on law and policy. When there's a current debate over a piece of legislation, for example tax reform or the FISA act, Lawfare offers a non-partisan view of what's in the bill and how it's likely to be interpreted.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Cass Sunstein on the Citizen's Guide to Impeachment

Partially Examined Life

The Partially Examined Life is a philosophy podcast by some guys who were at one point set on doing philosophy for a living but then thought better of it. They delve deeply into the works of key figures in philosphy.

Topic: Philosophy

Key Episode: Episode 19, Kant - What Can We Know? (Citizens Only)

Planet Money

Planet Money aims to make financial news both understandable and interesting to the lay public. They use creative storytelling to explain the complicated forces that move our economy.

Topic: Economics

Key Episode: Libertarian Summer Camp

Pod Save The World

After the Obama administration ended, some members of his team a podcast called 'Pod Save The World'. Tommy Vietor interviews former White House staffers and gets their take on current events from the perspective of people who have actually served in government.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: 8 Years in the Situation Room with Ben Rhodes

Politics and More

Journalists from the New Yorker talk to Dorothy Wickenden about current events. Look for interviews with Evan Osnos, Dexter Filkins, and John Cassidy in particular.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Why China Loves Trump

RadioLab

The podcast which gets people addicted to podcasts. If you've never listened to a podcast before, start with this one. Brilliant storytelling of fascinated topics in science and history, weaved together with emersive stereophonic sound.

Topic: Science

Key Episode: The Bad Show

Rationally Speaking with Julia Galef

\'Host Julia Galef explores the borderlands between reason and nonsense, likely and unlikely, and science and pseudoscience.\' Galef is a keen proponent of bayesian reasoning and uses a rationalist perspective to explores all sorts of topics. And she has the admirable ability of being able to coming up with concrete examples to explain nebulous concepts.

Topic: Science / Rationally

Key Episode: William MacAskill on 'Moral Uncertainty'

Talking Machines

Host Neil Lawrence (and former host Ryan Adams) talk to leading researchers about current developments in machine learning. The first segment of each episode, where a popular topic in ML (e.g., the bias-variance tradeoff) is explained is particularly valuable.

Topic: Machine Learning / AI

Key Episode: Hello World!

Ted Radio Hour

Ted talks are grouped together under a common theme and supplemented with additional interviews with the speakers. If you like Ted, you'll like Ted Radio Hour.

Topic: Science

Key Episode: The Unknown Brain

The Ezra Klein Show

Vox's Ezra Klein Show gives a left-wing take on current events and interviews some high profile guests, including Hillary Clinton, Malcolm Gladwell, Yuval Harari, and others.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: J.D. Vance, the reluctant interpreter of Trumpism

The Fifth Column

Kmele Foster, Matt Welch, Michael Moynihan offer their perspective on politics and race from a center, libertarian perspective. I mainly listen for Kmele Foster. Don't call him black.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Episode 84 w/ John McWhorter “Linguistics, Race, and Beyond"

The Gist

Master wordsmith Mike Pesca's daily take on the news. He has interesting guests from across the political spectrum and always offers a unique insight into current events. Plus he's funny.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Everybody, Somebody, Nobody

The Glenn Show

Over the last ten years, Brown University professor Glenn Loury has been conversing with Columbia University professor John McWhorter on the fraught question of race in America. Their conversations are unlike most you're likely to hear on this subject.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Talking Ta-Nehisi Coates

The New Yorker Radio Hour

Current editor of the New Yorker magazine David Remnick chats with journalists, politicans, and celebrities about current events.

Topic: Politics / Culture

Key Episode: Nicolás Maduro on the Brink of Dictatorship

Think Again

\'Spontaneous talk on surprise topics.\' Host Jason Gots asks his guests to respond to clips of other leading thinkers.

Topic: Politics / Science / Culture

Key Episode: Lawrence Krauss – Lux Ex Machina – Think Again Podcast

This American Life

The most popular podcast of all time. Ira glass and his team tell touching fictional and non-fictional vignettes of life in America. They also do longer explanatory pieces on, say, the financial crisis or the healthcare debate.

Topic: Culture

Key Episode: Harper high school

Very Bad Wizards

A psychologist who is interested in philosophy argues with a philosopher who is interested in psychology. Occasionally they make progress.

Topic: Philosophy

Key Episode: The Perils of Empathy (with Paul Bloom)

Waking Up

Sam Harris rejects the division of human inquiry into distinct subjects like 'science', 'politics', or 'philosophy.' He has wide ranging discussions on subjects both contemporary and historical, and emphasizes the importance of reason to live a life that maximizes human wellbeing.

Topic: Science / Politics / Philosophy

Key Episode: The Lessons of Death

WeThePeople Live

Host Josh Zepps has 'bar-room conversations' with people from across the political divide. His goal is to 'make debate healthy again', and he's a model for what healthy debate should look like. And he has an Australian accent.

Topic: Politics

Key Episode: Ep.52 Sam Harris, Joe Rogan, Hannibal Buress - A conversation about conversations, Part 1

You Are Not So Smart

A podcast about cognitive biases and the numerous ways humans deceive themselves.

Topic: Science

Key Episode: Bayes’ Theorem
